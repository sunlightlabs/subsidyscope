Treasury Department

The Treasury Department's efforts have several components. The chart below provides funding levels for each of these programs - both funds released to date and funds authorized to be released in the future. The programs are authorized for different periods of time and receive different level of funding. Because several of these programs include terms that allow the government to receive a return on its investment, it will take time before the precise subsidy cost of each program can be determined. Go to our TARP Subsidies page for some initial estimates on subsidy levels through the Treasury's Troubled Asset Relief Program (TARP), and visit our TARP Disbursements page to view the TARP data by transaction, recipient and date.

Click on the TARP Warrants page for an interactive table that allows users to track certain Treasury Department investments under the Troubled Asset Relief Program (TARP) in real time. Subsidyscope also highlights a discrepancy between the way Treasury publicly described pricing for warrants and terms found in the final contracts with many TARP recipients, which may have resulted in Treasury receiving less favorable pricing on these warrants.

Hover your cursor over the name of each program (in bold) for a more detailed description of its terms.
